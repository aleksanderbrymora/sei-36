Muhammad al-Khwarizmi

The Father Of Algebra

About

One of the first Directors of the House of Wisdom in Bagdad in the early 9th Century was an outstanding Persian mathematician called Muhammad Al-Khwarizmi.

The word “algorithm” is derived from the Latinization of his name, and the word “algebra” is derived from the Latinization of “al-jabr”, part of the title of his most famous book, in which he introduced the fundamental algebraic methods and techniques for solving equations.

Conributions

Al-Khwarizmi's popularizing treatise on algebra presented the first systematic solution of linear and quadratic equations.
